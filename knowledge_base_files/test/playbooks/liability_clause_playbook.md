# Liability Clause Playbook

Status: Current

## Preferred Clause

Except for excluded claims, each party's total aggregate liability arising out of or related to the agreement should not exceed the fees paid or payable in the twelve months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

## Excluded Claims

The following may be excluded from the liability cap if commercially approved:
- Payment obligations
- Breach of confidentiality
- Misuse of intellectual property
- Fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence
- Indemnification obligations for third-party IP infringement

## Review Notes

A contract is high risk if it includes unlimited liability for ordinary breach, indirect damages without exclusion, or a liability cap based on a very small amount such as one month of fees.

A liability cap of six months of fees may be acceptable only for low-value, low-risk beta agreements under $10,000 annual contract value and with no personal data processing.
