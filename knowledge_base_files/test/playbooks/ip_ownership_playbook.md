# Intellectual Property Ownership Playbook

Status: Current

## Default Position

Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property. Customer owns customer data and customer-provided materials. Vendor owns the platform, software, tools, templates, generic know-how, workflows, and improvements derived from generalized learnings.

## Work Product

For professional services, customer may own final deliverables specifically created for and paid for by customer, excluding vendor background technology, reusable components, templates, methods, and know-how.

## Red Flags

Escalate if a contract says:
- Customer owns all inventions, discoveries, improvements, or technology developed during the relationship.
- Vendor assigns all platform improvements to customer.
- Customer receives source code ownership or unrestricted sublicensing rights.
- Work product ownership transfers before payment is complete.

## Acceptable Compromise

Customer may receive a perpetual, worldwide license to use final deliverables for internal business purposes, while vendor retains ownership of background technology and reusable tools.
