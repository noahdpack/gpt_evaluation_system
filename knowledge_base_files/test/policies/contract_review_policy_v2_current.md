# Contract Review Policy v2 - Current

Status: Current
Effective date: 2025-08-01
Owner: Legal Operations

## Baseline Review Rules

All contract reviews must identify the agreement type, parties, effective date, term, renewal mechanics, payment obligations, termination rights, confidentiality terms, data protection terms, intellectual property ownership, liability cap, indemnity, governing law, venue, and assignment restrictions.

## Required Escalation Triggers

Escalate to legal counsel if any of the following are present:
- Unlimited liability or liability cap above twelve months of fees
- Customer ownership of pre-existing vendor IP or platform technology
- Broad indemnity covering indirect damages, lost profits, or customer business decisions
- Payment terms longer than net 60
- Auto-renewal without at least 30 days' prior written notice
- Governing law outside California, New York, Delaware, or England and Wales
- Data processing obligations without a corresponding DPA
- Non-compete, exclusivity, or most-favored-customer clauses
- Termination for convenience unavailable to the customer in professional services agreements over $50,000

## Preferred Positions

- Liability cap: twelve months of fees paid or payable under the agreement.
- Confidentiality term: at least three years after termination; trade secrets protected as long as legally protected.
- Payment terms: net 30 preferred; net 45 acceptable; net 60 requires business approval; longer than net 60 requires escalation.
- Governing law: California preferred for vendor paper; New York or Delaware acceptable. England and Wales acceptable for UK-based counterparties.
- Venue: state or federal courts located in the selected governing law jurisdiction.
- IP: each party retains pre-existing IP; customer owns customer data; vendor owns platform, tools, templates, know-how, and general improvements unless expressly negotiated otherwise.
